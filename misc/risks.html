Technology Risks

Digital simulations and serious gaming are expanding areas of education with potential benefits in terms of student engagement, motivation, and learning outcomes. However, the effectiveness of these tools lacks clear empirical evidence, leaving their adoption to the discretion of instructors and institutions. The absence of comprehensive policies and guidelines for their integration further complicates their use. Additionally, the varied definitions and terminologies surrounding digital simulations and games create ambiguity in the field. These challenges call for more research, evidence-based studies, standardized frameworks, and clear terminology to facilitate the effective integration of digital simulations and serious gaming into education.
