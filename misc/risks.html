Technology Risks

Digital simulations and serious gaming are an interesting and expanding area of education. They offer potential benefits in terms of student engagement, motivation, and learning outcomes. However, there's a dearth of clear empirical evidence regarding their effectiveness, and thus, the adoption of these tools largely falls on the instructor or institution's discretion. The lack of a comprehensive policy or guidelines for their integration into educational systems is also a challenge. Furthermore, the varied definitions and terminologies surrounding digital simulations and games create some "terminological ambiguity," which further complicates the field

The risks associated with the use of digital simulations and serious games in education include the following:

1. Online Rudeness: The anonymity of the digital world can lead to inappropriate behavior, which can emotionally impact users. However, this also presents an opportunity to teach students about online etiquette in a controlled environment.

2. Barrier to Physical Play: Digital games and simulations are often sedentary activities, and their use may take away from physical play and exercise, which are important for students' health.

3. Addictive Nature of Games: Many games are designed to be addictive, which can lead to excessive screen time and potential problems related to game addiction.

4. Curriculum Alignment: Not all games are designed with educational goals in mind. It's important to ensure that the games used align with the curriculum and support students' learning objectives.

5. High Costs of Equipment and Digital Divide: Digital technologies are not universally accessible. The cost of equipment can create a digital divide, where some students have access to these technologies, and others do not.

6. Over-Reliance on Technology: There's a risk that digital games and simulations could do too much of the thinking for the student, which could potentially hinder their learning.
